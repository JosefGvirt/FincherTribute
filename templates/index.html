Fincher trivia

  • David Fincher was fascinated with filmmaking from the age of eight, when he began making films with an 8mm camera. He also directed plays, designed sets and lighting after school, and worked as a non-union projectionist at a theater and as a production assistant at a local television news station​​.

  • Fincher directed numerous music videos, including Madonna's "Express Yourself" in 1989 and "Vogue" in 1990, both of which won him the MTV Video Music Award for Best Direction. He considered the production of music videos as his own "film school," where he learned to work efficiently within a small budget and time frame

  • Fincher received Academy Award nominations for Best Director for "The Curious Case of Benjamin Button," "The Social Network," and "Mank." He also directed other notable films like "The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo" and "Gone Girl"​​.
